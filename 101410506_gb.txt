The college was established during the formation of Ontario's community college system in 1967. Colleges of Applied Arts and Technology were established on May 21, 1965. The college is named after George Brown, who was an important 19th-century politician and newspaper publisher (he founded the Toronto Globe, forerunner to The Globe and Mail) and was one of the Fathers of Confederation.
The college was founded in 1967 as part of the creation of Ontario's community college system. On May 21, 1965, Colleges of Applied Arts and Technology were founded. George Brown, a significant 19th-century politician and newspaper publisher who established the Toronto Globe, the precursor to The Globe and Mail, and a founding member of the Confederation, is honoured by the college's name.
In addition to customized programs and services for recent immigrants and international students, George Brown offers more than 170 full-time programs in the following fields: business, community services, art and design, construction and engineering technologies, health sciences, hospitality and culinary arts, early childhood education, and preparatory studies.
