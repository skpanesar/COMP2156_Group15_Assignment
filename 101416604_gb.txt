With three campuses located in downtown Toronto, Ontario, Canada, the George Brown College of Applied Arts and Technology is a public, fully accredited institution of applied arts and technology. George Brown College was chartered by the Ontario government in 1966 and opened its doors the following year, much like many other institutions in the province.
In addition to customized programs and services for recent immigrants and international students, George Brown offers more than 170 full-time programs in the following fields: business, community services, art and design, construction and engineering technologies, health sciences, hospitality and culinary arts, early childhood education, and preparatory studies.
Situated on Kendal Avenue between Davenport Road and MacPherson Avenue is the Casa Loma campus. The City of Toronto Archives and Casa Loma are two nearby attractions. The campus consists of five different structures.
